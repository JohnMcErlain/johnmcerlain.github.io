Grassroots Web Development

Passion Igniting Possibility

2017-06-28
Grassroots Web Development

There are many good programmers out there… guys who know how to slice and dice the data, move it around, display it…guys who are really good technicians. Most never experience that flash of genius that connectss two, seemingly unrelated entities. Few have the passion which can ignite the growth of a data/code base with the potential to benefit many.

Most trends in society are commerce driven and the internet is no exception. Much of the web is composed of buyers and/or sellers. But there are also communities sprouting up everywhere. And while marketers may reduce a community to a set of buyers, members of that community seek information relevant to their group. Information is the gold rush of the 21st century (and no doubt will remain so for centuries to come).

Information is constructed from data and in fact does not exist until data is linked/viewed in a purposeful way. Prior to computers, data had to be collected manually, written down, and analyzed. The analysis might discover relationships and trends in the data, thus becomming information. Computers have expedited that process. We now input massive amounts of data, write programs to discover potential relationships and trends, and have the results returned in milli seconds, beautifully displayed on a browser interface!

The opportunities for connecting information on the internet are limitless bound only by our imaginations. Fortunately, the generous millennials (I’m not one) have steered humanity to an open model of sharing code, known as the open source movement. Whereas proprietary models of development (of past centuries) thwarted engagement and therefore imagination, the open source movement has sparked involvement and imagination. As a result, companies and institutions are exposing their data via application programming interfaces (APIs) to anyone who might find ways to connect with it.

So here we are with all this data and open source code. If you haven’t already staked your claim on the internet, the first step is to create a database and connect it to the vast ocean of data already accessible on the internet. There is no telling where such connections may lead us.
