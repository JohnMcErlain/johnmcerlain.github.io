Data Management: The Smart But Ugly Cousin

2014-02-07
College Relics

Contrasting structure vs format is somewhat akin to contrasting data management (DM) vs content management (CM). In DM, the constructs are well defined and precise. “Data” are raw facts. Information is derived from entity relationships based on predicate logic and mathematical set theory. This contrasts with CM where data are “snippets” and information are “components”, presented in the “eye of the beholder”. Content as structure is seemingly reduced to randomness and bias, flying rampantly out of control around the web. Then, formatted, content is arranged, shined up and “pizazzed” to present beautifully.

It’s almost as though DM is CM’s ugly cousin. DM, precise and documented, functionally delivering operational information, but lacking style and social grace…What can we do about it? Dress him/her up? Format him/her?

Whether or not you like the metaphor, one thing is certain… your computer won’t like or dislike it, because a computer can’t understand it. In fact if you direct your computer to search and return all metaphors relating to, let’s say “content management”, your computer will simply return all the documents containing the words “metaphor” and the phrase “content management”. This really seems to be the essence of the “content” vs “information” debate. Content requires bias, attitude, perspective, all of which a computer doesn’t possess.

Yet we would like our computer to do our slave labor for us. We want our computer to search and find relevant information, deliver it to our website, organize and present it, all without us lifting a finger. It’s almost as though we hired a very capable employee who is great at handling one thing (data) but cannot deliver meaningful bodies of data (knowledge) without a lot of guidance.

And knowledge is what we seek. So how do we get our beautiful cousin to acquire some brains?
How do we make our ugly cousin, beautiful and social? It’s the old conundrum of brains vs beauty. It is the conundrum of structure vs format.

Structure is more than just where we place things on our website; it is how we structure the data so other sites and/or users can easily communicate with us. Our “formatted” beautiful cousin, may look great on our site, and flatter us with smiles and one line musings, but can h/she communicate well with us? With other sites?

Take Craigslist.org for example.The entire site is constructed in a table format. As such, the structure has the capability of lending information to other sites. It’s structure makes it very easy for human users to quickly see job postings or locate used and often oddball shopping items. It is formatted in the style of the old newspaper “want ads”. Far from our beautiful cousin, but very smart, helpful and capable. This is clearly a case where I would prefer the ugly cousin.
