Sam Nelson Concept Applied to 'God'

2013-09-13
College Relics

On to Sam Nelson. I get his point. You can hone your mental prowess, develop logical thought processes, and proceed to artfully engage in debate with people who perceive the world through faith based initiatives. For instance, we might expand the theorem “There is no largest counting number” and apply it to the (‘concept’) entity of ‘God’. Our theorem might be “There is no God”. Let’s say God = n. The creation of God (God’s creator) might then be n + 1. God’s God’s creator might be n + 2 and so on. Every choice of the ultimate God is wrong, which is to say one’s choice of God is arbitrary.

On the other hand, the politician’s theorem might simply be “There is God”. No math is involved but the proof is simple: US Presidents always end an inaugural speech with the phrase “God Bless America”. Because a President (with the possible exception of n + 1 president’s starting with George Washington = n) would never lie, we must assume the presence of God, and it follows that “There is God”.

The same logic employed by the theorem “There is no largest counting number” could be used against Sam Nelson in his pro Scientific Theory argument. For instance, proponents of the theory of evolution may be affronted by those who denounce “it is only a theory”. While this particular theory represents our current state of cumulative evidence and analysis, it nonetheless is ‘always’ subject to modification with new evidence ( n+1 ) . While DNA analysis may clearly demonstrate our ascendence from the Apes, it is always subject to (yes, even significant) modification with new discovery. Nelson, himself, offers an example of this when the paradigm for the force of gravity shifted from Newton’s to Einstein’s model.

Ultimately, we must use our best judgement utilizing our greatest resources of logic and knowledge. If we argue against “global warming” by saying “it is just a theory, we potentially doom mankind. Do we operate on logical or faith based initiatives?

I once went to a college where the motto was “In Deo Speramus”. The translation is “In God We Hope”. The implication is that we have a great deal of responsibility for our destiny. Sam Nelson points out that Math is instrumental with this responsibility.
